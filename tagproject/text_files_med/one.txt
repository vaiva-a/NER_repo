The 45-year-old male patient presented with persistent chest pain and shortness of breath. 
His medical history includes hypertension and Type 2 diabetes mellitus. 
A physical examination revealed elevated blood pressure (150/95 mmHg) and an irregular heartbeat. 
The ECG showed signs of myocardial ischemia, and blood tests indicated elevated troponin levels. 
The patient was immediately started on aspirin, atorvastatin, and metoprolol. 
A coronary angiography was scheduled to assess possible arterial blockages.
